Meet the Author

👋

Trinity Renee Gibbs, who goes by Renee, is a fourteen year old poet and student at West Ashley High School. She writes both rhyming and free verse poetry, drawing on a childhood that was not always easy, one that held real hardship, real fear, and real loss, and turning that material into work that insists on hope, wit, and self-reclamation rather than despair. These four trilogies trace that journey: learning to take up space after being underestimated, finding composure and humor in the face of betrayal, reclaiming the frightened child she once was, and finally speaking as a witness to everything she has seen and survived. Renee reads mostly thriller and mystery, listens to R&B, hip hop, rap, soul, and blues, and one day hopes to publish a full poetry collection. This page is the beginning of that dream. And she thanks those who read, for supporting it.

Trilogy I

Blueprint of an Underdog

1.

Blueprint of an Underdog

Of all the children in my school, I may not be the strongest, Of all the voices in the world, my silence lasts the longest. Yet hidden deep within my chest, a fire starts to grow, With secrets and melodies the world has yet to know

For still my dreams can lead the way, beyond the walls I see. With hope and courage by my side, i trust in who i'll be So do not be quick to turn a blind eye Im gathering the strength it takes to spread my wings and fly

You only see a quiet face because I've just begun But every underdog will have their moment in the sun No longer hidden by the shadows of a heavy past I'm stepping forward into a light that's always built to last

I am like the sun because i shine so bright Everywhere i go i emit all different types of light A golden horizon that cannot be bound Leaving a trail of hope on the ground

With every step i take the world is spun They watch in awe at what i have become So underestimate me if you dare Im taking up my space, im standing there

You tried to mute my voice and lock the gate But you could never seal away my fate You tried to box me in and build a cage But now i rise and turn a new page

Im rewriting the plot, im changing my pace Yeah you've just started but im winning this race I lock onto targets, my vision is clear I banish the doubt and conquer every fear

I aim my potential like a loaded gun No obstacle exists that i will shun I shatter the boundaries to make them all see And strike like a lightning bolt, wild and free

To claim every triumph i've chased for many a year I stand in the light where the truth is sharp and near Look close at my face, because my moment is finally here The critics can whisper but i refuse to disappear

The shackles are broken, the damage undone I step into a future where no shadows can reappear My journey continues beneath a permanent sun The story is mine and the victory is clear

2.

When the Room Went Cold

Loss after loss, I have lost so many Loss after loss feels like plenty They packed up their promises and walked out the door Leaving me counting the cracks on the floor

I watched the tail lights fade into the gray Wondering why nobody ever chooses to stay The silent state left is a heavy cold room A graveyard of friendships that died in the bloom

I sat with my shadow deleting the names Tired of playing their disposable games You think that you are valued you think that you matter Then you watch as the mirrors and promises shatter

But just when the gravity started to pull You stepped in the wreckage and made the room full You looked past the armor and noticed my pain And stood right beside me to block out the rain

For the first time in forever I'm lifted off my feet Finally visible finally complete I'm sweeping the ashes left behind Reclaiming the pieces you thought I wouldn't find

They wanted me broken they wanted me small But you gave me a shoulder to climb over the wall The doorways are open the darkness is gone I'm no longer waiting for a fictional dawn

Surrounded by people who choose to remain I rise from the valley I stepped through the pain The losses are numbers I no longer count I am no longer lonely I'm finally found

3.

From Shadows to Solitude

I built a fortress out of quiet spaces Far from the noise of the competing faces Inside my chest a sudden stillness grew A private shelter that they never knew

I light a candle where the wind once blew And cultivate a peace that feels brand new I look back at the roots that bruised my feet, Not with regret but knowing I'm complete

The people who walked out or threw their stones Are just ghost in the past that no one owns I hold no anger for the things they said I simply chose to clear the path ahead

The warmth of honest praise will always help my spirit grow It guides my feet along the path and sets heart aglow I shift the rooms energy and elevate their view To leave a proud impression on the lives I'm passing through

But when I step inside my room and shut the heavy door I know my own internal peace is worth so much more I forgive the versions of me that didn't know How to protect my heart or let things go

The times I stayed when I should have walked away All the silent words I didn't say The past is a teacher not a cage And i am finally wise enough to turn the page

I tell the child inside me they are safe tonight No longer needing to prepare for flight or fight The Schoolyard whispers can no longer reach The sacred wisdom that the Silence has to teach

I wrap my younger self in a steady grace And tell them they belong in every single space The storm has cleared the air is clean and still No heavy clouds hangover on the hill

I sit in silence with a cup of tea Content with who is sitting here with me The ticking clock no longer feels like dread Just a steady rhythm for the days ahead

I traced the morning sunlight on the floor And realize I don't need a miracle or more The sound of rain against the window pane Is proof that life washes away the stain

I find a hidden wealth in being still A joy that money never will My energy is precious and my time is gold A beautiful story waiting to unfold

I hold my ground with courage as my shield Standing firmly in the open field I pull the weeds of doubt out by the root And plant the seeds of better fruit

I water thoughts of confidence and pride And let the flower open up inside The landscape of my soul is green and bright A peaceful garden growing in the light

They thought that leaving me would make me weak And leave me in a valley dark and bleak But isolation was a hidden door That taught me who I was and so much more

The quiet didn't break me,it restored my soul And took the broken pieces making them whole I give myself the grace i used to hide And stand as my own guardian and guide

I heal the wounds of who i used to be And fall in love with my own company I am the author and the pages mend My own best protector and my own best friend

Hand in hand with who i am today I step into the future, so come what may

Trilogy II

Canceling the Season

1.

Canceling the Season

They say hell is hot and they're probably right A burning heavy fever in the middle of the night But i have felt a colder fire freezing in my chest The sting of your betrayal was a different kind of test

The flames can scorch the surface, but ice will lock the gate You left me out in the winter just to calculate my fate So let the furnace roar away, im adapting to the chill, You couldn't break my spirit then and you never will

I must admit your grand betrayal had a bit of style It almost made me drop my jaw and miss a smile You thought you blew my world apart and left me in the dust But you just gave my schedule a much - needed adjust

I appreciate the drama and the theater that you brought But you are simply not as clever as you originally thought I gave your little exit scene a solid four out of ten The acting was a bit dramatic, please don't try again

You slammed the door so heavily to make a massive point But you just highlighted the flaws of this entire joint I hope you enjoyed the audience you gathered for the show Because im canceling the season, darling its time for you to go

You thought your absence left a crater gaping in the floor Like id be weeping every evening right beside the door But honestly , the quiet room is such a sweet relief I didn't even have time to cultivate a grief

The space you left is perfect for a brand new coffee table It turns out holding up your ego made me unstable So bless your heart for trying to freeze me in my place Im sitting here with perfect warmth and laughing in your face

The winter that you left me in was just a passing breeze It takes a lot more force than yours to make a giant freeze Go tell the world you broke my heart and make your story grand While i enjoy the absolute best summer in the land

I should probably send a bill for all the time i spent Listening to your complaints acting like they meant A single shred of anything to anyone but you- I guess i should thank you for the clarity i grew

You thought you were the main event, the highlight of the night But you were just the opening act that wasn't very bright I looked around for all the damage you said you'd leave I even brought some tissues just in case i had to grieve

But i checked the mirror carefully and everything is fine In fact my skin is glowing and ive never looked so divine Im sorry that your catastrophe fell totally flat But I've got better things to do than argue about that

You went around and told the town that you're the one who won Like you're the gravity that holds the Earth around the sun But your just a background character who stepped over their line A temporary shadow on a diamond built to shine

I change the locks, changed my number, changed the whole design And looking back the best part of my life was when you left the line So keep on whispering my name to anyone who'll hear Im living completely rent -free in your head throughout the year

You tried to make a tragedy but made a comedy instead You thought you left me broken, but your ego was misled I'd wish you well but honestly I don't care where you go Thanks for the free entertainment, i think i'll end the show

2.

The Waitlist

You treated me like backup; an option on the shelf While you were busy chasing after everyone but myself But now your plans have fallen flat and things are looking gray And suddenly you're memory-laneing back into my day

I love how our convenience makes you suddenly remember The person that you had frozen way back in December You expect a grand reception, and an open smiling face Like i've been sitting waiting for you to re-enter the space

Im sorry to inform you that the waitlist has been closed The vacancy you thought you had has been fully exposed I filled the space with better things, with progress and pride And didn't leave a single chair for you to sit inside

Go take your sudden loneliness to someone who will care Because my empathy for you is running thin and bare It must be deeply shocking when the tables turn And you become the afterthought you wanted me to learn

So bless your heart for knocking on a door made of stone Im far too busy thriving to answer on the phone I hope you enjoy the quiet of the bridge that you burned Consider this a master class in lessons highly learned

3.

The Carbon Copy

You roll your eyes publicly at what i display Then secretly imitate me the very next day You claim that my style is a bit overdone Then run to the mirror to copy line one

It must be exhausting to play the harsh judge While holding a deeply unoriginal grudge You trash my ideas then make them your own But a thief is just empty when left all alone

You sit in the corner and study my pace Convinced you can enter and win my race You repeat all the phrases and steps that i take But you're just a cheap and hollow remake

A shadow can never outshine the frame So changing your posture won't alter your name Im driving the engine your stuck in the back Like cheap imitation cargo derailed on the track

I am the designer the premium brand The highest quality goods in the land Your strictly the replica priced in the bin A plastic duplicate that's way too thin

I come with a lifetime guarantee of success You come with a glitch and a whole lot of stress Your a dollar store copy, you need to confess Im a luxury standard you cannot possess

Trilogy III

Splitting the Spine

1.

Splitting the Spine

At midnight i enter the gates of my mind To look for the pieces i left behind The clock strikes the hour, the world goes to sleep While i tend to the secrets i promise to keep

The roses have thorns as sharp as knives Each flower a memory carved by my life I walk through the midnight where yesterday grows And speak to the ghost that only my soul knows

I walk down a path overgrown by the years And find a small child who is drowning in fears They tremble in silence, too fragile to speak A ghost of compliance submissive and meek

They shrink from the world and pull down their head And swallow the words they wished they had said They sit in the corner with nowhere to run Defenseless and silent, out matched and outdone

They let people push them and take up their space Too scared of the conflict to look in their face They traded their boundaries just to keep the peace And waited for pressures that never would cease

A silent bystander to their own defeat Retreating in shadows to clear out the street They let others dictate the path and clear the track With no solid courage to never push back

2.

The Greenhouse of Expectations

I walk through a dome made of iron and glass Where shadows of cages are cast on the grass The air is suffocating, artificial and tight A trap disguised to modify my light

Inside they have planted a million constraints A garden of rules and historical complaints They tell me exactly the way i should grow How high i can reach and how much i can know

They clip at my leaves if i branch out too far And try to convince me to hide away my scar They want a neat flower to place on display But i am a wildfire they cannot convey

I look at the glass that is shielding the lie A barrier built just to limit the sky Im tired of breathing their temperature rules And playing in the garden of fools

The roots that they wanted to stifle and bind Are breaking the floorboards they left me behind I gather my strength and i stand up so tall And throw a heavy stone at the perimeter wall

The iron begins to fracture and shake As all the glass expectations start to break A brilliant explosion of shards in the night As i reclaim the space for my natural light

I step through the wreckage and breathe in the cold No longer a product bought or sold The wilderness inside me is ready to run No longer a flower afraid of the sun

Let the glass fall and shatter where it may Im growing completely wild today

3.

A Lease on Silence: Timber and Stone

They tailored their smile just to soften the room Apologizing for taking up space in the gloom Caring the weight of what everyone thought And paid for a peace that could never be bought

Thinking that they blended right into the wall They'd escape from the pressure of taking the fall Their voice was a whisper that choked in their throat A passenger trapped on a rudderless boat

Watching as others dictated the game They nod in agreement while swallowing shame Its heavy to look at the kid i once was Who gave up their power without a just cause

But here in the midnight, beneath the dim light I see how those roots were preparing to fight The grass that was bending began to take hold Deep down in the dirt where the weather was cold

Trembling in fear, the spineless seedling stood near Quietly waiting for me to appear I reach through the shadows and take by the hand The child who felt like they never could stand

I plant their feet upon a mountain of stone And tell them they will never be alone I give them the spine that was stolen away To stand like a giant and face down the day

I weave every boundary i've recently built Deep into their posture to banish the guilt I tell them it's okay to push back the crowd To take up their space and to speak out loud

At last the leg they were missing is found Cemented in courage and locking the ground The moment I touch them the garden transforms Roses stop bleeding and weather the storms

The weeds of compliance are pulled by the root And vines of authority offer new fruit Free from their fear they look in my eyes And see the bright future they're destined to rise

Back to the wreckage they run for the frame And try to contain me by shouting my name But you cannot cage what is meant to be wild The rules of the greenhouse are no longer filed

My branches are reaching my leaves are untamed A beautiful force that can never be shamed I laugh at the climate they thought they could control While I was busy reconstructing my soul

They wanted a specimen neat and polite But I'm a forest that swallows the night The artificial weather they manufactured to please Is blown far away by a natural breeze

I look at the iron now twisted and bend The lease on my silence has officially spent Rooted in freedom im standing up tall Outside the constraints of the perimeter wall

The sky is wide open the horizon is clear Im blooming completely without any fear Together we walk back to face down the night No longer two pieces but one single light

The kid with no backbone is shielding my chest A permanent proof that i passed the test The clock strikes the ending,the shadows depart I have grown a whole forest inside of my heart

Trilogy IV

The Diary of a Black Girl

1.

The Diary of a Black Girl; What She Sees, What She Knows Better

Everyday life Alters its face But the Casual tie stay frozen in the same exact place She walks past the bodies heavily sedated by grief Wondering what thief promise them this brief relief

They call it a temporary exit short-lived Escape But she has seen the ink stain the irreversible shape Once the needle for the Habit writes itself into your frame There is no eraser you are never the same

They think she is too young to the decode the damage on the street Assuming her eyes just watch the rhythm of her feet But she carries an older calculus deep inside her spine She knows better than to step across that blurred and broken line

They drink a liquid promise to just to make the memories fade But she has seen the heavy price of every choice they've made So she presses ink to paper while the sirens softly scream Writing down the waking truth to save her own tomorrow's dream

No potion or poison will ever dictate her worth She will not bury her magic in the concrete of this Earth Let the world stay blinded by the smoke and gasoline She is the author of the future keeping her vision clean

She will lock her crown in place far above the city grime Pulling light from the dark surviving one page at a time Because she knows her story is a mountain they can never climb

2.

The Mirror of a Black Girl; What She Hides, What She Heals Through

She bolts the bathroom door to strip the armor from her frame And stares into a silver lens that calls her by her name Behind the steady mask she wears to navigate the crowd Lies a suffocating quiet that is getting far too loud

She traps the bitter saltwater before it hits her cheek Anchoring the trembling wrists the world assumes are weak They demand a flawless fortress when they look into her eyes But the glass detects the baseline where the heavy shadow lies

Yet mending is a battlefield a slow and patient art Of gathering the broken flint to spark a frozen heart She traces her reflection where the frost begins to clear Breathing life back into spaces that were hollowed out by fear

With every study exhale she begins to rewrite the rules Converting all her history from weight to working tools The mirror is no longer a witness to her scars It's the midnight laboratory where she counts her internal stars

The quiet reflection ends, and the anthem now begins

3.

The Anthem of a Black Girl; What She Speaks, What She Holds Sacred

No longer hidden by her scars she opens up her eyes To see the twinkling stars ignite across the midnight skies One by one they wake the dark beginning now to shine As she steps into the light and says "I finally see it's mine"

She strips away the heavy cloak to everyone's design Unearthing diamonds in a space they thought was just a mine And takes her rightful place along the cosmic borderline Her voice becomes a tidal wave a thunder in the room

Shattering the silence where they thought she'd only bloom She speaks a heavy truth that makes old foundations shake Reclaiming every promise that they tried so hard to break For she has locked away the things she always holds sacred

A soul untouched by malice and a joy untouched by hatred No longer just a witness to the fire and the grime She stands a living monument triumphant in her time The architecture of her pain is turned to solid gold

A brand new manifestation of a story yet untold With gasoline in glass she built a kingdom from the spark Forever burning brilliant as a beacon in the dark
